Robotics Courses
System Controls
Graduate Level

This course dealt with the various control law synthesis techniques. A stong focus was put on techniques based on state estimators and state feedbacks (modal, LQG, ...), and on the synthesis of robust control laws ( H ∞ , H 2 , ...) . Moreover, this course showed how to apply the Pontryagin maximum principle to the generation of trajectories for dynamic systems (minimum time, minimum consumption,...). Finally, this course covered techniques used for synthesizing control laws adapted to non-linear systems .

Work samples:

  • LQG Control
  • Hinf Control
  • Modal Control

Perception and Navigation
Graduate Level

Autonomous navigation is the ability for a mobile system to navigate in its environment without the intervention of a pilot. This capacity requires the system to perceive and model its environment , to locate itself, and to plan the movements to be made. The objective of this course was to learn the tools and methods used for performing these functions, with a strong focus on localization, which plays a central role.

Work samples:

  • 2D SLAM
  • Autonomous Car Navigation

Planning and Decision
Graduate Level

Decision-making is required whenever an autonomous system has to deal with a diversity of tasks and environments and when its behavior cannot be entirely programmed in advance. This course dealt with the techniques used to provide an autonomous system with decision capabilities. Additionally, this course highlighted the ethical issues raised by the use of robots.

Work samples:

  • Implementation of RRT on a Robotic Arm

UAVs
Graduate Level

This course provided us with a multidisciplinary technical understanding of a drone system. We had the opportunity to grasp the jobs, missions, industrial challenges and technical constraints related to UAV systems. Practical sessions have made us aware of the right choices to make when designing drones. This course covered civil and military drones, ranging from microdrones to MALE or HALE surveillance drones, as well as combat drones (UCAV).

Work samples:

  • GNC software design for a quadcopter

Estimation
Graduate Level

This course dealt with the methods to use to best recover relevant information contained in a signal x(t) received by a sensor (or a network of sensors). The objective was to give us a strong knowledge basis about the main principles of estimation and about the different methods available in order to be able to critically choose the most suitable one(s) for a given problem.

Work samples:

  • Particle Filter

Embedded systems: Design, Modeling, Architecture
Graduate Level

Robots and drones are autonomous systems composed of hardware and software that are organized into a logical architecture including functional, supervisory and decision-making levels. This course covered the main aspects to take into account when designing the architecture of an autonomous system and when implementing the algorithms.

System Representation and Analysis
Graduate Level

This course detailed the tools used for the representation and the analysis of dynamic systems. In this respect, the course covered the techniques used to address problems related to model minimality, to model reduction, to the representation of frequency and parametric uncertainties, and to the modelling of non-linear systems.

Modelling of Dynamic Systems and Signals
Graduate Level

The main objectives of this course was to characterize the response of linear systems to random signals and to detail identification techniques(characterization of system behaviour based on experimental data).
